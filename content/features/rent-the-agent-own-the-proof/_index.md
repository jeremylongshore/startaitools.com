Anthropic's Claude Tag drops a strong agent into Slack with zero infrastructure. The trade you accept: the substrate is theirs. Sandbox, memory, audit log. Governance is coarse (channel scope plus spend caps), and the record is theirs to show you, not yours to verify offline.

CCSC and AGP make the opposite trade: fine-grained deterministic policy, per-tool-call human-in-command approval, and a signed audit you check with your own public key. No vendor in the trust path. Both are legitimate. Pick by who must own the substrate.
